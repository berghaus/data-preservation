Large Hadron Collider (LHC)

Started up in 2008, the LHC consists of a 27-kilometre ring of superconducting magnets with a number of accelerating structures to boost the energy of the particles along the way.

ATLAS, CMS, ALICE and LHCb are the four main detectors of the LHC. Three smaller detectors also exist and are used for more specific research: TOTEM, MoEDAL and LHCf.

It permitted the discovery of the Higgs boson, which was announced on 4 July 2012 by ATLAS and CMS experiments.
